# DOCID:legal_0001 | Article I Section 1

Article I Section 1
All legislative Powers herein granted shall be vested in a Congress of the United States, which shall consist of a Senate and House of Representatives.

# DOCID:legal_0002 | Article I Section 2

Article I Section 2
The House of Representatives shall be composed of Members chosen every second Year by the People of the several States.

# DOCID:legal_0003 | Article I Section 3

Article I Section 3
The Senate of the United States shall be composed of two Senators from each State, chosen for six Years; and each Senator shall have one Vote.

# DOCID:legal_0004 | Article I Section 7

Article I Section 7
Every Bill which shall have passed the House of Representatives and the Senate shall, before it become a Law, be presented to the President of the United States.

# DOCID:legal_0005 | Article I Section 8 Commerce Clause

Article I Section 8 Commerce Clause
The Congress shall have Power to regulate Commerce with foreign Nations, and among the several States, and with the Indian Tribes.

# DOCID:legal_0006 | Article I Section 8 Necessary and Proper Clause

Article I Section 8 Necessary and Proper Clause
The Congress shall have Power to make all Laws which shall be necessary and proper for carrying into Execution the foregoing Powers.

# DOCID:legal_0007 | Article I Section 9 Habeas Corpus

Article I Section 9 Habeas Corpus
The Privilege of the Writ of Habeas Corpus shall not be suspended, unless when in Cases of Rebellion or Invasion the public Safety may require it.

# DOCID:legal_0008 | Article II Section 1

Article II Section 1
The executive Power shall be vested in a President of the United States of America.

# DOCID:legal_0009 | Article II Section 2 Commander in Chief

Article II Section 2 Commander in Chief
The President shall be Commander in Chief of the Army and Navy of the United States.

# DOCID:legal_0010 | Article II Section 2 Treaty Power

Article II Section 2 Treaty Power
The President shall have Power, by and with the Advice and Consent of the Senate, to make Treaties, provided two thirds of the Senators present concur.

# DOCID:legal_0011 | Article III Section 1

Article III Section 1
The judicial Power of the United States shall be vested in one supreme Court, and in such inferior Courts as the Congress may establish.

# DOCID:legal_0012 | Article III Section 2

Article III Section 2
The judicial Power shall extend to all Cases, in Law and Equity, arising under this Constitution, the Laws of the United States, and Treaties made.

# DOCID:legal_0013 | Article IV Full Faith and Credit

Article IV Full Faith and Credit
Full Faith and Credit shall be given in each State to the public Acts, Records, and judicial Proceedings of every other State.

# DOCID:legal_0014 | Article IV Privileges and Immunities

Article IV Privileges and Immunities
The Citizens of each State shall be entitled to all Privileges and Immunities of Citizens in the several States.

# DOCID:legal_0015 | Article VI Supremacy Clause

Article VI Supremacy Clause
This Constitution, and the Laws of the United States which shall be made in Pursuance thereof, shall be the supreme Law of the Land.

# DOCID:legal_0016 | First Amendment

First Amendment
Congress shall make no law respecting an establishment of religion, or prohibiting the free exercise thereof; or abridging the freedom of speech, or of the press.

# DOCID:legal_0017 | Second Amendment

Second Amendment
A well regulated Militia, being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms shall not be infringed.

# DOCID:legal_0018 | Fourth Amendment

Fourth Amendment
The right of the people to be secure in their persons, houses, papers, and effects, against unreasonable searches and seizures, shall not be violated.

# DOCID:legal_0019 | Fifth Amendment Due Process

Fifth Amendment Due Process
No person shall be deprived of life, liberty, or property, without due process of law.

# DOCID:legal_0020 | Fifth Amendment Self Incrimination

Fifth Amendment Self Incrimination
No person shall be compelled in any criminal case to be a witness against himself.

# DOCID:legal_0021 | Sixth Amendment

Sixth Amendment
In all criminal prosecutions, the accused shall enjoy the right to a speedy and public trial, by an impartial jury.

# DOCID:legal_0022 | Eighth Amendment

Eighth Amendment
Excessive bail shall not be required, nor excessive fines imposed, nor cruel and unusual punishments inflicted.

# DOCID:legal_0023 | Tenth Amendment

Tenth Amendment
The powers not delegated to the United States by the Constitution, nor prohibited by it to the States, are reserved to the States respectively, or to the people.

# DOCID:legal_0024 | Fourteenth Amendment Equal Protection

Fourteenth Amendment Equal Protection
No State shall deny to any person within its jurisdiction the equal protection of the laws.
